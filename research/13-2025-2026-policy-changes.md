# 2025-2026 Immigration Policy Changes Affecting International Students & Workers

**Date:** April 7, 2026 | **Scope:** Federal executive actions, final/proposed rules, fee changes, agency guidance, state-level developments

---

## 1. Trump Administration Executive Orders (2025-2026)

### Presidential Proclamation: $100,000 H-1B Fee (Sep 19, 2025)

President Trump signed a proclamation titled "Restriction on Entry of Certain Nonimmigrant Workers" imposing a **$100,000 one-time fee** on all new H-1B petitions filed after September 21, 2025. Key details:

- Applies to new H-1B petitions only (not renewals, extensions, or transfers)
- Does not affect current H-1B holders traveling in/out of the U.S.
- Scheduled to expire September 21, 2026, unless extended
- DHS may issue national-interest exemptions (physician exemption under consideration)
- **Upheld** by federal court in December 2025

**Impact:** Dramatically increases employer cost for new H-1B sponsorship. Entry-level positions and smaller employers are disproportionately affected. Combined with the weighted lottery (see below), this creates a two-layer filter against lower-wage H-1B petitions.

### Executive Order: Enhanced Vetting (Jan 20, 2025)

Mandates increased vetting and screening for all individuals seeking U.S. admission, including F-1, J-1, M-1, and H-1B visa holders. Key provisions:

- **Social media screening** for all visa applicants and benefit requestors (formalized April 9, 2025)
- Accounts must be set to public and fully disclosed
- Consular officers examine posts for hostility toward U.S. institutions or support for terrorism/antisemitism
- Beginning December 15, 2025, H-1B applicants at consulates abroad also subject to online-presence review

### Visa Revocations and SEVIS Terminations

- ~450+ student visas revoked as of mid-2025 under "prudential revocation" authority
- Secretary Rubio announced plans to "aggressively revoke" Chinese student visas, particularly those with CCP ties or studying critical fields
- U.S. State Department briefly paused F/M/J visa processing in May 2025; resumed June 18, 2025 with expanded vetting
- Harvard and other institutions filed lawsuits challenging enforcement scope

### Travel Restrictions (Jan 1, 2026)

Full or partial suspension of entry and visa issuance for nationals of **39 countries**.

## 2. H-1B Modernization Final Rule (Effective Jan 17, 2025)

Published December 18, 2024 in the Federal Register. Major provisions:

| Area | Change |
|------|--------|
| **Beneficiary-centric registration** | One registration per beneficiary per fiscal year, regardless of how many employers register that person |
| **Specialty occupation definition** | Revised to clarify "normally" does not mean "always"; employer may accept a range of degree fields, but field(s) must be directly related to job duties |
| **Site visits** | Codifies USCIS authority to conduct physical, telephonic, or electronic site visits; refusal may result in denial or revocation |
| **Cap-gap extensions** | Provides automatic extensions of F-1 status and EAD for cap-gap beneficiaries |
| **Nonprofit/government cap exemption** | Clarifies which nonprofit and governmental research organizations qualify |

Sources: [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/12/18/2024-29354/modernizing-h-1b-requirements-providing-flexibility-in-the-f-1-program-and-program-improvements) | [Holland & Knight](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/01/positive-changes-for-business-immigration-the-h-1b-modernization-rule) | [USCIS Alert](https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/alerts/h-1b-final-rule-h-2-final-rule-and-revised-form-i-129-effective-jan-17-2025)

## 3. H-1B Weighted Selection (Wage-Based Lottery) Final Rule

Published December 29, 2025; **effective February 27, 2026**. Replaces the random lottery with a wage-weighted system.

| Wage Level | Description | Selection Pool Entries | Est. Selection Probability |
|------------|-------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Level IV | Leadership/expert roles | 4x | ~61% |
| Level III | Senior, complex duties | 3x | ~46% |
| Level II | Mid-level professional | 2x | ~31% |
| Level I | Entry-level/training | 1x | ~15% |

**Impact on recent graduates:** Level I positions (typical for new graduates on OPT transitioning to H-1B) see selection probability drop from ~30% historically to ~15%. Combined with the $100K fee, this effectively prices out most entry-level H-1B sponsorship.

First registration period under new system: **March 4-19, 2026** (FY 2027 cap).

Sources: [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/29/2025-23853/weighted-selection-process-for-registrants-and-petitioners-seeking-to-file-cap-subject-h-1b) | [Fisher Phillips](https://www.fisherphillips.com/en/news-insights/dhs-releases-final-h-1b-weighted-selection-rule.html) | [Fragomen](https://www.fragomen.com/insights/united-states-dhs-issues-final-rule-to-replace-random-h-1b-cap-lottery-with-wage-level-based-weighted-system-for-h-1b-cap-allocation-in-time-for-implementation-in-early-2026.html)

## 5. STEM OPT Program -- Threats and Proposed Changes

**Current status:** STEM OPT remains active as of April 2026, but faces existential threats from multiple directions.

| Threat Vector | Status |
|---------------|--------|
| **Project 2025** recommendation to eliminate OPT as "not authorized by Congress" | Policy blueprint; no rule text yet |
| **USCIS Director Edlow** public pledge to "terminate" OPT (Jul 2025) | Statement of intent; no proposed rule |
| **DHS internal letter** (Jan 9, 2026) confirming re-evaluation of OPT scope and duration | Under review |
| **S. 2821** (Sen. Banks, R-IN, Aug 2025) to abolish OPT legislatively | Introduced; in committee |
| **H.R. 2315** (Rep. Gosar, R-AZ) parallel House bill | Introduced; in committee |
| **Duration of Status elimination** (see Section 6) would indirectly shorten OPT window | Proposed rule; comment period closed |

**Economic stakes:** Institute for Progress estimates OPT elimination could cause $220B-$440B in annual economic losses within 10 years.

**What to do now:** No final rule has been issued. Students currently on STEM OPT should maintain compliance, keep I-983 training plans updated, and monitor regulatory developments closely.

Sources: [Berardi Immigration Law](https://berardiimmigrationlaw.com/major-opt-reform-on-the-horizon-what-students-graduates-and-employers-should-know/) | [Erickson Immigration Group](https://eiglaw.com/dhs-confirms-review-of-opt-and-stem-opt/) | [The PIE News](https://thepienews.com/us-launches-re-evaluation-of-opt/)

## 6. Proposed Rules Affecting F-1 Students

### End of Duration of Status (D/S) -- Proposed Rule (Aug 28, 2025)

The most consequential proposed change for international students. Would replace open-ended "D/S" admission with fixed-term stays.

| Current Policy | Proposed Change |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Admitted for "duration of status" (D/S) | Admitted until program end date on I-20, max 4 years |
| 60-day post-completion grace period | Shortened to 30 days |
| Unlawful presence accrues only after formal USCIS finding | Unlawful presence accrues automatically when admission period expires |
| No extension filing needed | Must file formal extension with USCIS for programs exceeding 4 years (PhD, medical) |
| Program changes allowed | Graduate F-1 students banned from changing programs |
| No biometrics for status maintenance | Possible biometrics requirement added |

**Comment period:** Closed September 29, 2025 (rule text); October 27, 2025 (paperwork/SEVIS comments).

**Impact:** PhD students (5-7 year programs) would need to file extensions. Any lapse triggers unlawful presence, potentially triggering 3/10-year bars. Creates massive administrative burden on university DSOs and USCIS alike.

Sources: [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/08/28/2025-16554/establishing-a-fixed-time-period-of-admission-and-an-extension-of-stay-procedure-for-nonimmigrant) | [Yale OISS](https://oiss.yale.edu/news/dhs-proposes-to-replace-duration-of-status-with-fixed-periods-of-stay-for-f-j-nonimmigrants) | [USC OIS](https://ois.usc.edu/2025/08/29/what-you-need-to-know-proposed-change-to-f-1-and-j-1-visa-duration/)

## 7. Employment-Based Green Card Updates

### Current Backlog (as of April 2026)

- **Total backlog:** 1+ million people (including dependents) waiting in EB queues
- **Annual cap:** ~140,000 EB green cards/year with 7% per-country limit
- **China EB-2:** 5-7 year backlog
- **India EB-2/EB-3:** Multi-decade waits
- **Rest of World EB-1/EB-2:** Generally current as of April 2026
- **EB-3 India:** Showed meaningful forward movement; most other countries returned to "current"

### Legislative Proposals

**Dignity Act of 2025** proposes:
- Raise per-country caps from 7% to 15%
- $20,000 premium processing option for those waiting 10+ years
- Backlog elimination target by 2035
- Exempt spouses and children from EB counts

**Status:** In Congress; no passage yet. DHS plans EB-1/EB-2/EB-3 regulatory clarifications in 2026 but has not released text.

Sources: [Fragomen](https://www.fragomen.com/insights/green-card-backlogs-and-visa-bulletin-trends-what-employers-can-do-in-2025-and-beyond.html) | [Boundless Visa Bulletin](https://www.boundless.com/blog/visa-bulletin) | [Fwd.us](https://www.fwd.us/news/per-country-cap-reform-priority-bill-spotlight/)

## 8. USCIS Processing Times & RFE/Denial Trends

### Processing Times (as of early 2026)

- **Total pending cases:** 11+ million (as of July 2025 -- record high)
- **I-90 (green card renewal):** 8+ months (up from 4 months in 2025)
- **Each RFE adds:** 60-90 days to processing time
- **Premium processing** available for I-140 and certain I-129 categories

### H-1B Approval/RFE/Denial Rates

| Metric | FY 2025 | Trend |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Overall approval rate | 97.9% (406,349 of 415,275) | Stable-high |
| New employment approval rate | 97% | Stable |
| Extension approval rate | 98% | Stable |
| RFE rate (extensions/transfers) | 9% -> 30%+ | Sharp increase in early 2026 |
| Denial rate | <4% | Lowest in a decade |

**Key insight:** RFEs are surging but are more targeted than the 2018-2020 era. High RFE volume with low denial rates suggests USCIS is using RFEs as a screening/delay mechanism rather than a pathway to denial. But each RFE adds months of uncertainty.

Sources: [Manifest Law Processing Times](https://manifestlaw.com/blog/uscis-processing-times/) | [Manifest Law Approval Rates](https://manifestlaw.com/blog/h1b-approval-rates/) | [Waylit RFE Analysis](https://www.waylit.com/resources/h-1b-rfe-rates-2026-what-hr-leaders-need-to-know)

## 10. International Entrepreneur Rule Status

The International Entrepreneur Rule (IER) remains active but limited:

- Provides **parole** (not a visa or green card) for up to 30 months, renewable once
- **$1,000 parole fee** took effect October 16, 2025
- Investment threshold increased to **$311,071** for FY 2025 (annual inflation adjustments)
- Parole is not "admission" -- generally cannot adjust status from within the U.S.
- No indication the Trump administration plans to rescind the rule, but no expansion either

Sources: [USCIS IER Page](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/international-entrepreneur-rule) | [Loigica](https://loigica.com/international-entrepreneur-parole-not-green-card/)

## 11. State-Level Developments

### Restrictive States

- **Texas:** SB 8 (effective Jan 1, 2026) requires sheriffs to cooperate with ICE via 287(g); held 18,734 ICE detainees as of Feb 2026
- **Florida:** Expanded 287(g) agreements to 344 agencies by March 2026

### Protective States

- **California:** 2026 laws require schools/universities to notify communities when immigration enforcement is on campus; prohibit enforcement in certain areas without judicial warrant
- **22 states + DC** maintain tuition equity laws allowing qualifying students to pay in-state tuition rates

### Federal-State Tensions

Anti-DEI executive orders and funding threats create secondary risks for international students at public universities that depend on federal grants. Universities are navigating between federal compliance demands and state-level protections.

## Summary: What This Means for International Students

The 2025-2026 policy environment represents the most restrictive period for international students and H-1B workers since the first Trump administration. The key changes compound:

1. **Studying:** Fixed admission periods replace D/S; social media screening; visa revocations; SEVIS terminations
2. **Working (OPT):** Program under active DHS review; legislative proposals to eliminate it; no final rule yet
3. **H-1B transition:** $100K fee + wage-weighted lottery + rising RFE rates make entry-level sponsorship extremely difficult
4. **Green card path:** Multi-year backlogs persist; no legislative fix has passed; Dignity Act in committee
5. **Costs:** Fee increases across nearly every immigration form; $250 Visa Integrity Fee added

**Bottom line:** International students should (a) maintain meticulous immigration compliance, (b) plan for longer timelines and higher costs at every stage, (c) consider higher-wage or STEM career paths that improve H-1B selection odds, and (d) consult immigration counsel before any status change, travel, or employment decision.
