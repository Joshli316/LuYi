# OPT, STEM OPT Extension, and Cap-Gap Rules

*Research compiled April 2026. Immigration rules are evolving rapidly under the current administration; verify critical deadlines with a DSO or immigration attorney.*

---

## 1. Post-Completion OPT Basics

Post-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) grants F-1 students up to **12 months** of work authorization in a field directly related to their major area of study.

### Application Timeline

| Milestone | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Earliest filing date | 90 days before program end date |
| Latest USCIS receipt date | 60 days after program end date |
| I-20 must be submitted with application | Within 30 days of DSO recommendation date |
| Typical processing time | 90-150 days |

- The student must have been in valid F-1 status for at least one full academic year.
- Each higher degree level earns a new 12-month OPT eligibility (bachelor's, master's, doctorate).
- Any pre-completion OPT used is deducted from the 12-month total.

### 2026 Fee Update

The I-765 filing fee increased to **$1,780** (up from $1,685). As of mid-December 2025, USCIS may require in-person **biometric appointments** for new OPT and STEM OPT applications, adding processing time.

Sources: [USCIS OPT Page](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-opt-for-f-1-students), [USC OIS](https://ois.usc.edu/employment/employment-f1/opt/post-completion-opt/)

## 2. STEM OPT 24-Month Extension

F-1 students with degrees in STEM-designated fields may apply for a **24-month extension**, for a total of up to **36 months** of OPT work authorization.

### Eligibility Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Degree field | Must be on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List (CIP code on I-20) |
| Employer enrollment | Employer **must** be enrolled in **E-Verify** |
| Training plan | Completed Form I-983 signed by employer and student |
| Filing window | Up to 90 days before OPT expiration; must be filed before OPT expires |
| I-20 submission | Within 60 days of DSO recommendation in SEVIS |

### Eligible CIP Codes

DHS defines STEM fields broadly: engineering (CIP 14), biological sciences (CIP 26), mathematics (CIP 27), physical sciences (CIP 40), and **related fields**. The list was last expanded in 2023 to include data science, cybersecurity, and other emerging fields. Students should verify their specific CIP code against the [DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/eligible-cip-codes-for-the-stem-opt-extension).

### Automatic 180-Day Bridge

If the STEM OPT extension application is filed on time and the initial OPT period expires while the extension is pending, employment authorization is **automatically extended for 180 days** while USCIS adjudicates.

### Form I-983 Training Plan

- Employer completes Sections 3-6 describing learning objectives, compensation, hours, and supervision plan.
- **12-month evaluation** due within 10 days of the first anniversary of the STEM OPT start date.
- **Final evaluation** due within 10 days of the end of the STEM OPT period.
- Any **material change** (employer EIN change, compensation reduction, significant hour reduction, changed objectives) requires a new I-983 filed with the DSO at the earliest opportunity.
- Employer must report student's termination or departure to the DSO within **5 business days**.

Sources: [USCIS STEM OPT](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-extension-for-stem-students-stem-opt), [Study in the States](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/stem-opt-extension-overview), [Form I-983 Overview](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/form-i-983-overview)

## 3. Unemployment Limits

| OPT Type | Maximum Cumulative Unemployment |
|---|---|
| Post-completion OPT (12 months) | **90 days** |
| STEM OPT extension (additional 24 months) | **150 days total** (includes any days from initial OPT) |

- Every calendar day (including weekends) without qualifying employment counts.
- Unemployment clock starts on the OPT start date printed on the EAD, not the first day of work.
- Exceeding the limit can result in **automatic SEVIS termination**, loss of F-1 status, loss of the 60-day grace period, and denial of future immigration benefits.
- Unemployment during a **cap-gap extension** also counts toward these limits.

Sources: [Study in the States Unemployment Counter](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-help-hub/student-records/fm-student-employment/unemployment-counter), [Stanford Bechtel Center](https://bechtel.stanford.edu/navigate-international-life/employment/f-1-student-employment/optional-practical-training-opt-0)

## 4. OPT Reporting Requirements

### SEVP Portal Obligations

All OPT holders must report the following within **10 days** of any change:

- Employer name and address (use work-site address; if fully remote, use home address)
- Employment start and end dates
- Description of how work relates to major/field of study
- Personal name or address changes
- Any period of unemployment

### STEM OPT Additional Reporting

- Annual self-evaluation via Form I-983 at 12 months
- Final evaluation at the end of the STEM OPT period
- Material changes to the training plan require a new I-983
- Employer must report termination within 5 business days

### Consequences of Non-Reporting

If employment is not updated in the SEVP Portal within **90 days** of the EAD start date, the SEVIS record may be automatically terminated.

Sources: [Yale OISS](https://oiss.yale.edu/employment-taxes/employment-for-international-students/f-1-students/optional-practical-training/opt-reporting-requirements), [Cornell International Services](https://international.globallearning.cornell.edu/employment-and-taxes/report-your-opt-employment)

## 5. Cap-Gap Provisions

The "cap-gap" bridges the period between an F-1 student's OPT/status expiration and the October 1 H-1B start date.

### Eligibility

All of the following must be true:

1. Student is in valid F-1 status (on approved OPT, STEM OPT, or in the 60-day grace period as of April 1)
2. Student has not violated immigration status
3. A timely-filed, **cap-subject** H-1B petition requests **change of status** (not consular processing)
4. The H-1B petition requests an employment start date in the relevant fiscal year

### New Rule: Extension to April 1 (Effective January 17, 2025)

| Scenario | Cap-Gap End Date |
|---|---|
| **Previous rule** | October 1 of the relevant fiscal year |
| **New rule (FY2026 onward)** | **April 1** of the following fiscal year |

This means an F-1 student with a pending FY2027 H-1B petition filed in spring 2026 can maintain status and work authorization through **April 1, 2027** (or until the petition is approved/denied, whichever comes first).

### Cap-Gap Termination Triggers

The cap-gap extension ends **immediately** if:

- The H-1B petition is denied, withdrawn, rejected, or revoked
- The student is found to have a status violation, fraud, or misrepresentation

**Cap-exempt employers** (universities, nonprofit research organizations, government research facilities) are not eligible for cap-gap because their H-1B petitions are not subject to the cap.

Sources: [USCIS Cap-Gap](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/temporary-workers/h-1b-specialty-occupations/extension-of-post-completion-optional-practical-training-opt-and-f-1-status-for-eligible-students), [Study in the States Cap-Gap Rule](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/2025/04/recent-h-1b-rule-extends-f-1-cap-gap-extension)

## 6. What If H-1B Is Not Selected?

If the employer's H-1B registration is **not selected** in the lottery:

- **No cap-gap extension** is available (cap-gap requires a filed petition, not just a registration).
- The student's OPT/STEM OPT continues on its own terms until its expiration date.
- After OPT/STEM OPT expires, the standard **60-day grace period** applies.

If the H-1B petition was selected and filed but later **denied**:

- Cap-gap terminates immediately.
- A 60-day grace period begins (unless denial was based on fraud/misrepresentation, in which case no grace period).

Sources: [Smart Investor Visas](https://www.smartinvestorvisas.com/h-1b-cap-gap-in-2026-what-f-1-students-need-to-know), [USCIS Cap-Gap](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/temporary-workers/h-1b-specialty-occupations/extension-of-post-completion-optional-practical-training-opt-and-f-1-status-for-eligible-students)

## 7. 60-Day Grace Period

After OPT or STEM OPT expires (and the unemployment limit has not been exceeded), F-1 students receive a **60-day grace period**.

### Permitted Activities

- Prepare for departure from the United States
- Transfer SEVIS record to another SEVP-certified school
- Change education level (e.g., start a new degree program)
- File a change of status application with USCIS (e.g., to H-1B, O-1, etc.)

### Restrictions

- **No work authorization** during the grace period
- **No re-entry** to the U.S. if you depart during the grace period
- Grace period is **forfeited** if unemployment limits were exceeded or if USCIS finds fraud/misrepresentation

### Proposed Change (August 2025 NPRM)

DHS proposed reducing the F-1 grace period from **60 days to 30 days** as part of the duration-of-status reform. This is a proposed rule, not yet finalized as of April 2026.

Sources: [USCIS Policy Manual Ch. 5](https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5), [Manifest Law](https://manifestlaw.com/blog/f1-visa-grace-period/), [Study in the States](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/2019/07/students-understand-your-post-completion-grace-period)

## 8. Multiple H-1B Attempts on STEM OPT

STEM OPT's 24-month extension gives students up to **3 chances** at the H-1B lottery:

| Attempt | Fiscal Year Example | Cumulative Selection Probability* |
|---|---|---|
| 1st (during initial OPT or Year 1 of STEM) | FY2027 | ~35% |
| 2nd (Year 1-2 of STEM OPT) | FY2028 | ~58% |
| 3rd (Year 2 of STEM OPT) | FY2029 | ~73% |

*Based on historical random lottery rates. The FY2027 lottery onward uses a **wage-weighted selection system** (finalized December 29, 2025, effective February 27, 2026), which favors higher prevailing wage levels. Actual odds now depend on the offered salary level relative to other registrations.

Sources: [Scale.jobs OPT to H-1B Timeline](https://scale.jobs/blog/opt-to-h1b-timeline-international-students-2026), [GoElite H-1B Lottery](https://goelite.com/blogs/h-1b-visa-lottery-2026-fy-results-out-odds-changes)

## 9. Day 1 CPT Risks and USCIS Scrutiny

Day 1 CPT is **technically legal** under F-1 regulations but carries significant and growing risks in the 2025-2026 enforcement environment.

### How It Works

A transfer student enrolls in a new program at a school whose curriculum requires immediate CPT participation. This allows full-time work authorization from the first day of enrollment.

### Red Flags That Trigger Scrutiny

- Programs conducted mostly or entirely online
- Minimal on-campus attendance (weekend or hybrid formats)
- Marketing that emphasizes work authorization over academics
- High volume of CPT approvals relative to program size
- Weak documentation linking CPT to academic requirements

### Documented Consequences

- **H-1B RFEs** requesting syllabi, transcripts, attendance records, and proof that CPT was academically required
- **H-1B denials** for students who cannot document legitimate academic use
- **SEVP site visits** at schools with high Day 1 CPT volumes have increased
- **Travel risks**: port-of-entry scrutiny is higher; immigration attorneys currently advise Day 1 CPT students to avoid unnecessary international travel
- Potential finding that the student was **not maintaining valid F-1 status**, which can affect all future immigration applications

Sources: [Prodigy Finance Day 1 CPT Guide (2026)](https://prodigyfinance.com/resources/blog/day-1-cpt-universities-risks-rumours-and-what-uscis-actually-says-2026-updated-guide-/), [PSBP Law](https://psbplaw.com/blog/the-risks-of-day-1-cpt-under-the-new-trump-administration/), [CPTDog](https://cptdog.com/day-1-cpt-risk)

## 10. Options After STEM OPT Expires with No H-1B

If STEM OPT expires and no H-1B petition is approved, options include:

| Option | Key Details |
|---|---|
| **Enroll in a new degree program** | Resets F-1 status; earns new OPT eligibility at a higher degree level |
| **O-1 visa (extraordinary ability)** | No lottery, no annual cap; requires meeting 3 of 8 USCIS criteria; employer/agent must petition |
| **Cap-exempt H-1B** | Work at a university, nonprofit research org, or government research facility; year-round filing |
| **L-1 intra-company transfer** | Requires 1 year of employment abroad with a multinational employer |
| **Employment-based green card** | Employer sponsors EB-2 or EB-3; long processing times, especially for Indian/Chinese nationals |
| **Entrepreneur options** | E-2 treaty investor (requires treaty country nationality) or O-1 with self-petitioned agent |
| **Leave the U.S. and work abroad** | Gain experience; potentially return via L-1, H-1B consular processing, or O-1 |

The 60-day grace period after STEM OPT expiration is the window to execute any of these transitions. No work is permitted during the grace period.

Sources: [ISOA Guide](https://www.isoa.org/blog/how-you-can-stay-in-the-u.s.-after-your-f-1-opt-expires), [Scale.jobs](https://scale.jobs/blog/h1b-lottery-2026-options-if-not-selected), [ALG Lawyers](https://www.alglawyers.com/blog-en/how-to-legally-remain-and-work-in-the-us-after-your-opt-expires/)

## 11. F-1 to O-1 Change of Status

The O-1 visa is a strong alternative for F-1 students who can demonstrate extraordinary ability or achievement.

### Timing Considerations

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| Who files | U.S. employer or authorized agent files Form I-129 |
| Regular processing | ~9.5-11 months (as of early 2026) |
| Premium processing | 15 business days; costs ~$2,805 |
| When to file | While in valid F-1 status or during OPT/60-day grace period |
| Can you work while pending? | Not until the O-1 is approved and effective |

### Practical Strategy

- Start building an O-1 evidence portfolio **early** during OPT/STEM OPT (publications, awards, press, high salary, judging roles).
- File the O-1 petition **before** OPT expires to maintain valid status for the change-of-status request.
- If the grace period has already begun, you can still file, but you cannot work while the petition is pending.
- **Premium processing is strongly recommended** given the tight timing windows.

Sources: [Manifest Law F-1 to O-1](https://manifestlaw.com/blog/f1-visa-to-o1-visa/), [Boundless](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/change-status-from-f1-visa-to-o1-visa), [Colombo & Hurd](https://www.colombohurdlaw.com/visas/o-1-visa/o-1-visa-processing-time/)

## 12. Recent Changes (2024-2026)

### Already in Effect

| Date | Change |
|---|---|
| **Aug 2024** | USCIS Policy Alert PA-2024-22: clarified STEM OPT rules on online study, school transfers, grace periods, study abroad |
| **Jan 17, 2025** | Cap-gap extension period extended from October 1 to **April 1** of the next fiscal year (applies from FY2026 onward) |
| **Aug 19, 2025** | Social media review policy: USCIS officers review applicants' online activity as part of vetting; anti-American or antisemitic content is a serious negative factor |
| **Dec 12, 2025** | New biometric/photo requirements for OPT filings; USCIS may require in-person biometric appointments |
| **Dec 29, 2025** | H-1B wage-weighted lottery selection finalized (effective Feb 27, 2026; first applies to FY2027 cap season) |
| **Jan 1, 2026** | "Hold and review" policy for I-765 applications from citizens of 39 travel-ban countries |
| **2026** | I-765 fee increased to $1,780 |

### Proposed (Not Yet Final as of April 2026)

| Proposed Change | Details |
|---|---|
| **End of Duration of Status (D/S)** | F-1 admission limited to program end date, max 4 years; grace period reduced from 60 to 30 days; restrictions on changing majors/programs. Published Aug 28, 2025. Comment period closed Oct 2025. Final rule pending. |
| **OPT program reform** | Broader executive review of OPT is ongoing; no specific proposed rule published yet, but policy signals suggest potential shortening of OPT periods or additional restrictions |

### Key Risk: Duration of Status Reform

If finalized, the D/S rule would fundamentally change F-1 planning:

- Students admitted for a **maximum of 4 years** (not "duration of status")
- Must apply for extensions beyond 4 years
- Grace period drops to **30 days** (from 60)
- Undergraduate students cannot change majors in their first year
- Graduate students cannot change programs at any point

This rule is **not yet in effect** but could be finalized in 2026.

Sources: [NAFSA D/S Proposal](https://www.nafsa.org/regulatory-information/dhs-proposal-replace-duration-status), [Immigration Fleet](https://immigrationfleet.com/articles/f-1-student-visa-2026-major-changes-to-duration-of-status-opt-deadlines-and-what-every-international-student-must-know/), [RNB Law](https://www.rnlawgroup.com/key-updates-to-opt-for-stem-students-on-f-1-visas/), [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/08/28/2025-16554/establishing-a-fixed-time-period-of-admission-and-an-extension-of-stay-procedure-for-nonimmigrant)

## Quick Reference: Critical Timelines

```
Program Ends
  |
  ├── Apply for OPT (up to 90 days before, no later than 60 days after)
  |
  ├── OPT Starts (12 months)
  |     ├── 90-day unemployment limit
  |     ├── Report employment within 10 days of changes
  |     ├── Apply for STEM OPT extension (up to 90 days before OPT expires)
  |     └── H-1B lottery attempt #1
  |
  ├── STEM OPT (24 additional months)
  |     ├── 150-day cumulative unemployment limit
  |     ├── I-983 training plan + 12-month & final evaluations
  |     ├── E-Verify employer required
  |     ├── H-1B lottery attempt #2
  |     └── H-1B lottery attempt #3
  |
  ├── Cap-Gap (if H-1B petition filed with COS)
  |     └── Extends status + work auth through April 1 (new rule)
  |
  └── 60-Day Grace Period (no work; may file COS, transfer, or depart)
        └── Proposed reduction to 30 days (not yet final)
```
