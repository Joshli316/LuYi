## 1. H-1B Cap Structure

The annual H-1B cap has remained unchanged since FY 2004:

| Category | Annual Allocation |
|---|---|
| Regular cap | 65,000 |
| Advanced degree exemption (U.S. master's or higher) | 20,000 |
| **Total cap-subject slots** | **85,000** |

Cap-exempt employers (universities, nonprofit research organizations, government research organizations) are not subject to these limits.

## 3. The Multiple Registration Fraud Problem & Crackdown

### What Happened

In FY 2023 and FY 2024, the H-1B registration system was exploited by companies and staffing firms filing multiple registrations for the same beneficiary through different entities. This inflated the registration pool and drove selection rates below 25%.

- **FY 2024 peak abuse:** 758,994 registrations for only ~422,000 unique individuals -- meaning many people had 2-5+ entries.
- USCIS identified patterns of shell companies created solely to file extra lottery entries.

### The "One Beneficiary, One Registration" Rule (FY 2025)

Effective for the FY 2025 cap season, USCIS implemented a beneficiary-centric selection process:

- **Selection by unique beneficiary**, not by registration. Each person gets one lottery chance regardless of how many employers register them.
- Beneficiary identity is tied to **passport or valid travel document number**.
- If multiple employers register the same person, USCIS randomly selects which employer's registration to associate with the selected beneficiary.

### Enforcement Actions

- USCIS is retroactively investigating FY 2023 and FY 2024 registrations.
- Consequences include petition **denial, revocation, and criminal referrals**.
- USCIS codified authority to deny or revoke any H-1B petition where registration attestations are found to be false or inaccurate.

### Impact

| Metric | FY 2024 (Pre-Reform) | FY 2025 (Post-Reform) | FY 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total registrations | 758,994 | 470,342 | 343,981 |
| Year-over-year change | -- | -38% | -27% |
| Selection rate | 24.8% | 28.7% | ~35% |

The dramatic drop in registrations confirms that a significant portion of FY 2024 volume was fraudulent.

Sources: [USCIS Integrity Measures](https://www.uscis.gov/archive/uscis-announces-strengthened-integrity-measures-for-h-1b-program), [RNB Law Group Fraud Crackdown](https://www.rnlawgroup.com/uscis-crackdown-on-multiple-h-1b-registrations-and-fraud-investigation/), [Federal Register Final Rule](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/02/02/2024-01770/improving-the-h-1b-registration-selection-process-and-program-integrity)

## 4. Major Rule Changes by Administration

### Biden Administration (2024 -- Jan 2025)

1. **H-1B Modernization Rule** (Final rule, effective Jan 17, 2025):
   - Beneficiary-centric selection (one person, one entry) starting FY 2025
   - Expanded definition of "specialty occupation"
   - Extended cap-gap protection for F-1 students from October 1 to **April 1** of the relevant fiscal year
   - Allowed entrepreneurs with ownership stakes to qualify for H-1B in certain cases
   - New organizational accounts for employers on myUSCIS

2. **Fee Adjustments** (April 2024):
   - Base I-129 filing fee increased to $780 (reduced rate $460 for small employers/nonprofits)
   - Registration fee increased to $215 per beneficiary
   - New Asylum Program Fee: $600 (large employers), $300 (small employers), $0 (nonprofits)

### Trump Administration (Jan 2025 -- Present)

1. **$100,000 Supplemental Fee** (Presidential Proclamation, Sept 21, 2025):
   - Applies to new H-1B petitions for beneficiaries **outside the U.S.** or requesting consular notification
   - Does **not** apply to: renewals/extensions, people already in H-1B status, or change-of-status petitions for people already in the U.S.
   - Payment required via pay.gov **before** filing the I-129
   - Physicians may receive exemptions (under consideration)
   - Set to expire September 21, 2026, unless extended

2. **Wage-Weighted Lottery** (Final rule, effective Feb 27, 2026, for FY 2027):
   - Replaces the random lottery with a wage-level-weighted selection
   - Entries per registration based on DOL wage level:

   | DOL Wage Level | Description | Lottery Entries |
   |---|---|---|
   | Level 1 | Entry-level (~17th percentile) | 1 entry |
   | Level 2 | Qualified (~34th percentile) | 2 entries |
   | Level 3 | Experienced (~50th percentile) | 3 entries |
   | Level 4 | Fully competent (~67th percentile) | 4 entries |

   - **Anti-gaming provision:** If a beneficiary has multiple registrations, they are assigned the **lowest** wage level among all registrations.
   - This structurally disadvantages entry-level positions (many new graduates) and advantages higher-paid, senior roles.

3. **H-1B and L-1 Visa Reform Act of 2025** (proposed legislation):
   - Additional scrutiny and potential caps on staffing/outsourcing companies
   - Still moving through Congress as of April 2026

4. **Proposed Prevailing Wage Overhaul** (DOL NPRM, March 2026):
   - Would raise prevailing wage thresholds from 17th/34th/50th/67th percentiles to **34th/52nd/70th/88th percentiles**
   - Public comment period open through May 26, 2026
   - Not yet finalized -- would significantly increase required salaries

Sources: [Mayer Brown H-1B Modernization Analysis](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2024/12/h1b-modernization-bidens-final-move-trump-first-challenge), [White House Proclamation](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/09/restriction-on-entry-of-certain-nonimmigrant-workers/), [Federal Register Weighted Selection Rule](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/29/2025-23853/weighted-selection-process-for-registrants-and-petitioners-seeking-to-file-cap-subject-h-1b), [Holland & Knight DOL Wage Proposal](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/dol-targets-prevailing-wages-sweeping-increases-proposed-for-h1b-h1b1)

## 5. H-1B Wage Levels & Prevailing Wage

### Current System (OFLC Wage Year July 2025 -- June 2026)

Wages are set using the Bureau of Labor Statistics' Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) survey, by occupation and geographic area:

| Level | Label | Percentile | Typical Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Entry | ~17th | New graduates, minimal experience |
| 2 | Qualified | ~34th | 2-5 years experience |
| 3 | Experienced | ~50th | 5-10 years, some specialization |
| 4 | Fully Competent | ~67th | Senior/expert level |

### Example: Software Developers (SOC 15-1252) in San Jose-Sunnyvale, CA

| Level | Approximate Annual Wage |
|---|---|
| Level 1 | ~$130,000 |
| Level 2 | ~$155,000 |
| Level 3 | ~$180,000 |
| Level 4 | ~$210,000 |

*Exact figures vary by OFLC determination period. Check [flag.dol.gov](https://flag.dol.gov/programs/prevailingwages) for current rates.*

### Implications of Weighted Lottery for International Students

Most new graduates file at **Level 1 or Level 2**, receiving only 1-2 lottery entries under the FY 2027 weighted system. Senior professionals at Level 3-4 receive 3-4 entries, giving them 3-4x better odds. This fundamentally shifts H-1B from an equal-access lottery to one that favors higher-paid workers.

Sources: [DOL Prevailing Wages](https://flag.dol.gov/programs/prevailingwages), [Alma H-1B Wage Level Rule](https://www.tryalma.com/learn/h1b-wage-level-rule), [EPI H-1B Wage Analysis](https://www.epi.org/publication/h-1b-visas-and-prevailing-wage-levels/)

## 6. Premium Processing

| Detail | Current (as of March 2026) |
|---|---|
| Fee | **$2,965** (increased from $2,805 on March 1, 2026) |
| Processing guarantee | **15 business days** from receipt |
| Outcome within 15 days | Approval, denial, or Request for Evidence (RFE) |
| RFE impact | Resets the 15-day clock |
| Filing method | Form I-907, paper only (cannot file online) |

## 7. H-1B Transfer & Portability

### Core Rule (AC21 -- American Competitiveness in the 21st Century Act)

- An H-1B worker can **start working for a new employer** as soon as the new I-129 petition is filed and a receipt notice is issued -- no need to wait for approval.
- The new petition must be **nonfrivolous** and include a certified Labor Condition Application (LCA).

### Eligibility Requirements

- Must be in valid H-1B status at the time of filing (or within the 60-day grace period after employment ends)
- New employer must submit: job offer, certified LCA, prior H-1B approvals, I-94, and recent pay stubs

### Key Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Work authorization during transfer | Authorized upon receipt of new petition |
| If new petition denied | Work authorization terminates immediately |
| 60-day grace period | After H-1B employment ends, worker has 60 days to find new sponsor, change status, or depart |
| $100,000 fee | Likely does **not** apply to domestic employer-to-employer transfers (applies to consular/overseas petitions) |
| Cap exemption | H-1B transfers are **cap-exempt** -- no lottery required |
| Processing time (standard) | 4-8 months |
| Processing time (premium) | 15 business days |

Sources: [VisaVerge H-1B Portability Guide](https://www.visaverge.com/news/h-1b-portability-in-2025-2026-how-to-change-employers/), [DOL Portability Fact Sheet](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/fact-sheets/62w-H1b-portability)

## 10. Cap-Gap Protection for F-1 Students

For international students transitioning from OPT to H-1B:

- **Cap-gap extension** automatically extends F-1 status and OPT work authorization if a timely H-1B cap-subject petition (with change of status) is filed.
- As of the Biden modernization rule (Jan 2025), cap-gap protection extends through **April 1** of the relevant fiscal year (previously only October 1).
- If the H-1B petition is denied, withdrawn, or not selected, cap-gap terminates and a **60-day grace period** begins (no work authorization during grace period).
- Students on STEM OPT who enter the 60-day grace period **cannot** apply for a new STEM OPT extension.

Sources: [USCIS Cap-Gap Extension](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/temporary-workers/h-1b-specialty-occupations/extension-of-post-completion-optional-practical-training-opt-and-f-1-status-for-eligible-students), [Study in the States Cap-Gap](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/2025/04/recent-h-1b-rule-extends-f-1-cap-gap-extension)

## 11. Bottom Line for International Students (2026)

1. **Odds are better than FY 2024** -- fraud crackdown cleaned up the pool, raising selection rates from ~25% back to ~35%.
2. **The weighted lottery changes everything for new graduates** -- Level 1 wages get 1 entry while Level 4 gets 4. Negotiate salary aggressively.
3. **The $100,000 fee primarily hits overseas applicants** -- students already in the U.S. on F-1/OPT requesting change of status are generally exempt.
4. **Prevailing wage increases are coming** -- if the DOL proposal passes, required salaries will jump significantly (e.g., Level 1 from 17th to 34th percentile).
5. **Cap-gap protection is stronger** -- extended through April 1, giving more buffer time.
6. **H-1B portability remains intact** -- once you have H-1B status, changing employers is straightforward and cap-exempt.
7. **Plan B matters** -- with sub-40% odds even in good years, having alternatives (STEM OPT extension, O-1, EB-1, employer in cap-exempt org) is essential.
