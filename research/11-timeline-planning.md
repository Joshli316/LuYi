# Immigration Timeline Planning for International Students

**Research Date:** April 7, 2026
**Scope:** End-to-end immigration timeline from freshman year through green card, with critical deadlines and month-by-month planning.

---

## 1. Undergraduate Years: Building the Foundation

### Freshman & Sophomore Year (Years 1-2)

The first two years are about strategic positioning -- no immigration filings happen yet, but choices made here determine which pathways open later.

**STEM Degree Planning**
- Choose a STEM-designated CIP code major. The [DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/eligible-cip-codes-for-the-stem-opt-extension) determines eligibility for the 24-month STEM OPT extension, which effectively triples your post-graduation work runway from 12 months to 36 months.
- Double majors or minors in STEM fields provide backup eligibility even if your primary degree is non-STEM.

**O-1 Evidence Building (Start Early)**
- The O-1A visa requires meeting at least 3 of 8 evidentiary criteria ([USCIS Policy Manual Vol. 2, Part M, Ch. 4](https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-m-chapter-4)). Evidence accumulation should begin freshman year:

| O-1 Criterion | Undergraduate Actions |
|---|---|
| Awards/prizes | Apply for departmental honors, national competitions, hackathons, research prizes |
| Scholarly publications | Co-author research papers; target peer-reviewed journals with impact factors |
| Judging others' work | Serve as peer reviewer for student journals or conference committees |
| Original contributions | File provisional patents, develop novel research methodologies |
| Membership in associations | Join selective honor societies (Phi Beta Kappa, Tau Beta Pi) |
| High salary / remuneration | Document paid research positions at above-average rates |
| Media coverage | Seek press coverage for projects, startup activities, or research breakthroughs |
| Critical role in distinguished orgs | Leadership in well-known labs, companies, or nonprofits |

**Practical Actions**
- Join a faculty research lab by sophomore year; aim for named authorship by junior year.
- Maintain a portfolio documenting all evidence (awards, publications, press mentions, letters of recommendation).
- Attend career fairs and note which employers sponsor H-1B visas.

### Junior Year (Year 3)

**Intensify Evidence & Begin Professional Networking**
- Target at least 1-2 peer-reviewed publications or conference presentations.
- Begin assembling recommendation letters from professors and industry contacts who can speak to "original contributions of major significance."
- Research immigration attorneys -- many offer free initial consultations. The goal is to understand your specific options (O-1 vs. H-1B vs. EB-2 NIW) before senior year job search begins.
- Identify employers who sponsor H-1B visas using databases like [MyVisaJobs](https://www.myvisajobs.com) or the DOL OFLC disclosure data.
- Apply for summer internships at H-1B-friendly employers to build relationships for full-time offers.

### Senior Year (Year 4)

**OPT Application Window**
- You may apply for post-completion OPT **up to 90 days before** your program end date and **no later than 60 days after** ([USCIS OPT Guidelines](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-opt-for-f-1-students)).
- **2026 Update:** The post-completion grace period has been reduced from 60 to **30 days** for taking action after program completion under new F-1 rules ([Immigration Fleet](https://immigrationfleet.com/articles/f-1-student-visa-2026-major-changes-to-duration-of-status-opt-deadlines-and-what-every-international-student-must-know/)).
- File Form I-765 within **30 days** of receiving your OPT I-20 from your DSO.
- Filing fee: **$1,780** (increased from $1,685 in 2026).
- Processing time: approximately 60-90 days. Plan accordingly so your EAD card arrives before your desired employment start date.

**H-1B Preparation**
- Secure a job offer from an employer willing to sponsor H-1B **before March** of your graduation year.
- The employer must register you for the H-1B lottery during the March registration window.
- FY 2027 (starting October 1, 2026) registration: **March 4-19, 2026** ([USCIS H-1B Registration](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/temporary-workers/h-1b-specialty-occupations/h-1b-electronic-registration-process)).

## 2. H-1B Lottery Timeline (Annual Cycle)

The H-1B follows a strict annual cycle. Here is the FY 2027 timeline (for October 2026 start), which repeats annually with minor date shifts:

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| **Jan-Feb** | Employer prepares registration; confirms job details, SOC code, wage level |
| **Mar 4-19, 2026** | Electronic registration window open; $215 fee per registration |
| **By Mar 31** | USCIS completes lottery selection and notifies |
| **Apr 1 - Jun 30** | 90-day window to file full H-1B petition (Form I-129) |
| **Oct 1, 2026** | Earliest H-1B start date for selected petitions |

### 2026 Weighted Lottery System (New)

As of FY 2027, USCIS replaced the random lottery with a **wage-weighted selection** system ([DHS Final Rule, effective Feb. 27, 2026](https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/news-releases/dhs-changes-process-for-awarding-h-1b-work-visas-to-better-protect-american-workers)):

| Wage Level | Lottery Entries | Selection Probability Change |
|---|---|---|
| Level I (entry) | 1x | -48% (significantly reduced) |
| Level II | 2x | +3% |
| Level III | 3x | +55% |
| Level IV (senior) | 4x | +107% |

**Implication for new graduates:** Most entry-level positions pay Level I wages, meaning first-time applicants face roughly half the selection odds compared to the old random system. This makes STEM OPT extension and alternative pathways (O-1, EB-2 NIW) more strategically important.

### Cap-Gap Extension

If you are on OPT and your employer files a timely H-1B petition requesting change of status:
- Your F-1 status and work authorization are **automatically extended** until April 1 of the next fiscal year (previously only until October 1) under the [2025 H-1B final rule](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/2025/04/recent-h-1b-rule-extends-f-1-cap-gap-extension).
- If the petition is denied, withdrawn, or not selected, the cap-gap extension terminates immediately.

## 3. Post-Graduation: Month-by-Month Timeline

The following timeline assumes a **May graduation** with a STEM degree. Adjust dates if graduating in December.

### Year 1 After Graduation

| Month | Action | Deadline/Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mar (before graduation)** | Employer registers you for H-1B lottery | Registration window ~Mar 1-19 |
| **Mar-Apr** | Apply for OPT (90 days before program end) | File I-765 + $1,780 fee |
| **May** | Graduate; program end date on I-20 | 30-day action window begins |
| **May-Jul** | Receive EAD card; begin OPT employment | Must start work within 90 days of EAD start date |
| **Jun-Jul** | If H-1B selected: employer files petition (I-129) | Due within 90 days of selection |
| **Oct 1** | H-1B status begins (if approved) | Otherwise continue on OPT |
| **Oct-Dec** | If not selected: continue OPT, build O-1 evidence, plan next lottery | No action required; monitor unemployment days |

### Year 2 After Graduation (OPT Year)

| Month | Action | Deadline/Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Jan-Feb** | Prepare for second H-1B lottery attempt | Employer re-registers in March |
| **Feb-Mar** | **Apply for STEM OPT Extension** | Must file I-765 up to 90 days before OPT expires, but **no later than OPT expiration date** |
| **Mar** | Second H-1B lottery registration | $215 fee |
| **Apr-May** | 12-month OPT expires; STEM OPT extension kicks in | If STEM OPT pending, you receive automatic 180-day extension of work authorization |
| **May onward** | Continue employment on STEM OPT (24 additional months) | Total: 36 months of work authorization |

### Years 2-4 After Graduation (STEM OPT Period)

| Month/Period | Action | Deadline/Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Every 6 months** | Submit validation report to DSO on Form I-983 | Required self-evaluation of training progress |
| **Each March** | Additional H-1B lottery attempts | Up to 3 total attempts during STEM OPT |
| **Year 2-3** | Employer initiates PERM labor certification (if pursuing EB-2/EB-3 green card) | Begin prevailing wage request 6-8 months before PERM filing |
| **Year 3** | PERM filed; processing takes ~16 months | Audit adds 3-5+ months |
| **Year 3-4** | I-140 filed after PERM approval | Regular: 6-9 months; Premium: 15 business days |
| **STEM OPT end** | Must have H-1B, O-1, or other valid status | 60-day grace period to depart or change status |

## 4. Green Card Pathways & Timelines

### Path A: Employer-Sponsored (PERM -> I-140 -> I-485)

| Stage | Timeline | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prevailing wage determination | 5-7 months | Must be obtained before recruitment |
| PERM recruitment & filing | 3-6 months prep + filing | 30-day job posting, Sunday newspaper ads, etc. |
| PERM processing | ~16 months (as of 2026) | [DOL processing times](https://manifestlaw.com/blog/perm-dol-processing-times/) |
| PERM audit (if triggered) | +3-5 months | ~30% of cases audited |
| I-140 petition | 6-9 months (regular) / 15 days (premium) | Must file within 6 months of PERM approval |
| I-485 adjustment of status | 8-24 months | If priority date is current |
| **Total (most countries)** | **3-5 years** | From PERM initiation to green card |
| **Total (India EB-2)** | **10-15+ years** | Severe backlog; priority dates from ~2013 |
| **Total (China EB-2)** | **4-6 years** | Moderate backlog |

### Path B: EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW) -- No Employer Needed

| Stage | Timeline | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Petition preparation | 2-4 months | Gather evidence, expert letters, draft petition |
| I-140 filing | Day 0 | Self-petitioned; no PERM required |
| I-140 processing | ~22.5 months (regular) / 45 days (premium) | [As of March 2026](https://www.tryalma.com/blog/eb2-niw-processing-time) |
| I-485 (if priority date current) | 8-18 months | File concurrently if dates are current |
| **Total (most countries)** | **1.5-3 years** | Significantly faster than PERM route |

**NIW advantages:** No employer sponsorship required, no PERM labor market test, can self-petition. Strong for STEM graduates with publications, patents, or demonstrable national interest contributions.

### Path C: O-1A Extraordinary Ability (Nonimmigrant Bridge)

| Stage | Timeline | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Evidence compilation | 3-6 months | Need 3 of 8 criteria |
| Petition filing | Regular: 2-4 months / Premium: 15 days | Employer or agent must petition |
| Validity | Up to 3 years, renewable | No lottery, no annual cap |

The O-1 is a **bridge strategy** -- it provides uncapped work authorization while you pursue a green card through EB-1A or EB-2 NIW.

## 5. Critical Deadlines Reference

| Deadline | Duration | What Happens If Missed |
|---|---|---|
| **OPT application window** | 90 days before to 60 days after program end | Lose OPT eligibility entirely |
| **OPT I-20 to I-765 filing** | 30 days from OPT I-20 issuance | Application rejected |
| **Post-graduation action window** | 30 days (reduced from 60 in 2026) | Fall out of status |
| **OPT unemployment limit** | 90 days during initial 12-month OPT | Status violation; must stop clock by finding employment |
| **STEM OPT unemployment limit** | 150 days total (90 initial + 60 extension) | Status violation; deportability |
| **STEM OPT filing deadline** | Up to 90 days before OPT expires; **no later than** OPT expiration date | Lose STEM OPT eligibility |
| **STEM OPT reporting** | Every 6 months (I-983 validation) | Status violation |
| **H-1B registration window** | ~2 weeks in early March (annually) | Miss that year's lottery entirely |
| **H-1B petition filing** | Within 90 days of selection | Forfeited selection |
| **Post-OPT grace period** | 60 days after EAD expiration | Must depart U.S. or change status; no work allowed |
| **Cap-gap extension** | Until April 1 of the next FY (new rule) | Terminates if H-1B denied/withdrawn |
| **I-140 after PERM** | Within 6 months of PERM approval | PERM expires; must restart |
| **I-485 filing** | When priority date is current per Visa Bulletin | Check monthly Visa Bulletin |

## 6. When to Start Each Process (Relative to Graduation)

| Process | When to Start | Lead Time |
|---|---|---|
| O-1 evidence building | Freshman year (-4 years) | Continuous; takes years to accumulate |
| STEM degree selection | Freshman year (-4 years) | Must be declared before graduation |
| Immigration attorney consultation | Junior year (-2 years) | Need time to evaluate options |
| Employer research (H-1B sponsors) | Junior year (-2 years) | Inform job search strategy |
| OPT application | 90 days before graduation (-3 months) | Processing takes 60-90 days |
| H-1B lottery registration | March before graduation (-2 months) | Requires job offer + employer action |
| STEM OPT extension | 90 days before OPT expires (~9 months post-grad) | Must file before 12-month OPT ends |
| PERM initiation | 1-2 years after starting employment | Employer-driven; begin conversation early |
| EB-2 NIW self-petition | Anytime after building sufficient evidence | Can file during OPT, H-1B, or O-1 |
| O-1 petition | When 3+ criteria are met (often 2-3 years post-grad) | 3-6 months preparation |

## 7. 2026 Regulatory Changes Summary

Several major rule changes affect the timeline in 2026:

1. **Fixed Admission Period:** F-1 students now admitted for max 4 years instead of duration of status. Extensions require Form I-539 filing with USCIS ([Federal Register, Aug 2025](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/08/28/2025-16554/establishing-a-fixed-time-period-of-admission-and-an-extension-of-stay-procedure-for-nonimmigrant)).
2. **Reduced Grace Period:** Post-completion action window cut from 60 to 30 days.
3. **Wage-Weighted H-1B Lottery:** Higher wages = better odds. Entry-level positions face ~48% reduced selection probability.
4. **Extended Cap-Gap:** Now extends to April 1 (from October 1), providing 6 additional months of work authorization for H-1B change-of-status applicants.
5. **Increased Fees:** OPT filing fee increased to $1,780; H-1B registration fee $215; potential $100,000 fee for consular-processed H-1B petitions (pending litigation).
6. **Biometric Requirements:** New in-person biometric appointments may be required for OPT/STEM OPT applications filed after mid-December 2025.
