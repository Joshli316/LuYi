# Employment-Based Green Card Categories: Comprehensive Comparison

*Research date: April 7, 2026 | For international students and early-career professionals*

---

## 1. EB-1A: Extraordinary Ability

**Overview:** For individuals with sustained national or international acclaim in sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics. The highest-prestige employment-based category.

**Key requirements:**
- One major internationally recognized award (Nobel, Pulitzer, Oscar, etc.), **OR**
- Meet at least **3 of 10 evidentiary criteria**:
  1. Nationally/internationally recognized prizes or awards
  2. Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
  3. Published material about you in professional/major media
  4. Judging the work of others in your field
  5. Original contributions of major significance
  6. Authorship of scholarly articles in professional journals
  7. Display of work at artistic exhibitions or showcases
  8. Leading or critical role in distinguished organizations
  9. High salary or remuneration relative to peers
  10. Commercial success in the performing arts
- Must intend to continue working in your area of extraordinary ability in the U.S.

**Self-petition:** Yes -- no employer or job offer required  
**Labor certification (PERM):** Not required  
**Typical evidence strength:** Meeting 4-5 criteria with strong documentation is more persuasive than weak evidence across 7+  
**USCIS evaluation:** Two-stage process -- first checks if criteria are met, then weighs totality of evidence holistically

---

## 2. EB-1B: Outstanding Professors and Researchers

**Overview:** For internationally recognized outstanding professors and researchers with at least 3 years of experience in their academic field.

**Key requirements:**
- At least **3 years** of teaching or research experience in the academic area
- Meet at least **2 of 6 criteria**:
  1. Major prizes or awards for outstanding achievement
  2. Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
  3. Published material by others about your work
  4. Participation as a judge of others' work
  5. Original scientific or scholarly research contributions
  6. Authorship of scholarly books or articles

**Self-petition:** No -- requires employer sponsorship  
**Employer must be:** U.S. university, institution of higher education, or private employer with 3+ full-time researchers and documented accomplishments  
**Job offer:** Must be for a tenured, tenure-track, or comparable permanent research position  
**Labor certification (PERM):** Not required

---

## 3. EB-1C: Multinational Manager or Executive

**Overview:** For managers/executives transferring from a foreign affiliate, subsidiary, or parent company to a U.S. office.

**Key requirements:**
- Employed abroad in a managerial or executive capacity for at least **1 of the past 3 years**
- Qualifying corporate relationship between U.S. and foreign entities (parent, subsidiary, affiliate, or branch)
- U.S. employer must have been doing business for at least **1 year**
- U.S. job must be in a managerial or executive capacity

**Role definitions:**
- **Managerial:** Supervises professionals, manages an essential function, has hiring/firing authority
- **Executive:** Directs a major component, sets policies, exercises wide discretion, receives only general supervision

**Self-petition:** No -- requires employer sponsorship  
**Labor certification (PERM):** Not required  
**Approval rate:** 85-95% for properly documented cases (2026)

---

## 4. EB-2 with PERM Labor Certification

**Overview:** For professionals with advanced degrees or exceptional ability, sponsored by a U.S. employer through the PERM labor certification process.

**Key requirements:**
- **Advanced degree** (master's or higher), OR bachelor's + 5 years progressive experience, OR exceptional ability in the field
- Employer must file PERM labor certification proving no qualified U.S. workers available
- Employer conducts structured recruitment (job ads, labor market testing)
- Position must be permanent and full-time
- Wage must meet or exceed the prevailing wage for that occupation and location

**Self-petition:** No -- requires employer sponsorship  
**Labor certification (PERM):** Required  
**PERM timeline:** 24-30+ months total (prevailing wage determination through I-140 approval); PERM adjudication alone averages 15+ months, audits add 3-5 months  
**Who pays:** Employer must cover all PERM-related expenses and I-140 filing fee

---

## 5. EB-2 NIW: National Interest Waiver

**Overview:** A special EB-2 pathway that waives the job offer and labor certification requirements because the applicant's work serves the U.S. national interest.

**Key requirements (two layers):**

**Layer 1 -- EB-2 qualification:**
- Advanced degree (master's or higher, or foreign equivalent), OR
- Exceptional ability in your field

**Layer 2 -- NIW three-prong test (Matter of Dhanasar):**
1. Your proposed endeavor has **substantial merit and national importance**
2. You are **well-positioned** to advance the proposed endeavor
3. It would be **beneficial to the U.S.** to waive the job offer and labor certification requirements

**Self-petition:** Yes -- no employer or job offer required  
**Labor certification (PERM):** Not required (waived)  
**Processing time:** ~22.5 months standard (as of March 2026); premium processing available (45 business days)  
**Popular with:** STEM researchers, engineers, physicians, entrepreneurs, academics

---

## 6. EB-3: Skilled Workers, Professionals, and Other Workers

**Overview:** The most accessible employment-based category, for workers with qualifying job offers from U.S. employers.

**Three subcategories:**

| Subcategory | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Skilled Workers** | Job requires 2+ years of training or experience |
| **Professionals** | Job requires a U.S. bachelor's degree (or foreign equivalent) |
| **Other Workers** | Unskilled labor requiring less than 2 years training (limited to 10,000 visas/year) |

**Self-petition:** No -- requires employer sponsorship  
**Labor certification (PERM):** Required for all subcategories  
**Typical total timeline:** 3-5 years (longer for India/China-born applicants)

## 8. Concurrent Filing (I-140 + I-485)

Concurrent filing means submitting Form I-140 (immigrant petition) and Form I-485 (adjustment of status) at the same time, rather than waiting for I-140 approval first.

**Eligible categories:** EB-1A, EB-1B, EB-1C, EB-2, EB-2 NIW, and EB-3 -- all employment-based preference categories.

**Requirements:**
- Your priority date must be **current** (a visa number must be immediately available)
- You must be physically present in the U.S. (adjustment of status only)
- For PERM-based categories, PERM must already be approved

**Benefits of concurrent filing:**
- File for **EAD (work permit)** and **Advance Parole (travel document)** immediately
- Begin accruing time toward the 180-day AC21 portability threshold sooner
- Faster path to work/travel flexibility while green card is pending

**As of April 2026:** USCIS confirmed use of the Dates for Filing (DFF) chart for all employment-based categories, broadening concurrent filing eligibility.

## 9. AC21 Portability: Changing Employers

The American Competitiveness in the Twenty-First Century Act (AC21) allows green card applicants to change employers without restarting the process.

**Three requirements:**
1. **I-485 pending 180+ days** -- the clock starts when USCIS receives your I-485
2. **I-140 approved** (or approvable when filed concurrently)
3. **New job in same or similar occupational classification** as the original petition

**Key rules:**
- USCIS does not use a simple SOC code match; they analyze whether the new position is in the same or similar occupational classification holistically
- Must file **Form I-485 Supplement J** to formally request portability
- **Do NOT switch before 180 days** -- your I-485 may be denied
- After 180 days, even if your former employer **withdraws the I-140**, your petition remains valid
- Applies to EB-1, EB-2, and EB-3 categories

**Practical impact:** AC21 portability is a critical safety net. Once your I-485 has been pending 180+ days, you have significant freedom to change jobs, negotiate better offers, or leave a bad employer without losing your green card progress.

## 10. Premium Processing Availability

Premium processing (Form I-907) guarantees USCIS will take action on your I-140 within a set timeframe.

| Category | Available? | Timeframe | Fee (as of Mar 1, 2026) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EB-1A** | Yes | 15 calendar days | $2,965 |
| **EB-1B** | Yes | 15 calendar days | $2,965 |
| **EB-1C** | Yes | 45 business days | $2,965 |
| **EB-2 PERM** | Yes | 15 business days | $2,965 |
| **EB-2 NIW** | Yes | 45 business days | $2,965 |
| **EB-3** | Yes | 15 business days | $2,965 |

> **Note:** "Action" means USCIS will approve, deny, or issue a Request for Evidence (RFE) within the timeframe. An RFE resets the clock. USCIS may temporarily suspend premium processing for specific categories during high-volume periods.

> **Important:** Premium processing is only for the I-140 petition. There is **no premium processing for I-485** (adjustment of status).

## Key Takeaways for International Students

1. **Best self-petition options:** EB-1A and EB-2 NIW are the only categories that let you file without an employer. EB-2 NIW is the more accessible of the two for most early-career professionals with a master's degree.

2. **Fastest path (if you qualify):** EB-1A with premium processing -- 15-day I-140 decision, current priority dates for most countries, no PERM wait.

3. **Most common path:** EB-2 or EB-3 with PERM through an employer. Expect 2-5 years total for rest-of-world; much longer for India/China.

4. **China-born applicants:** EB-1 has a ~3-year backlog; EB-2 has ~4.5 years; EB-3 has ~5 years. Consider EB-1A or EB-2 NIW to skip the PERM queue.

5. **India-born applicants:** EB-2 has a ~12-year backlog. EB-1 (~3 years) is vastly faster. Pursuing EB-1A is strategically important if possible.

6. **Dual filing strategy:** File EB-2 NIW (self-petition, no employer needed) while also having your employer file EB-2 or EB-3 with PERM. Use whichever gets current first.

7. **AC21 portability is your safety net:** Once I-485 has been pending 180 days, you can change employers freely (same/similar job). This protects you from being tied to one employer.

8. **Premium processing is universal for I-140:** All EB categories now support it. Use it strategically -- the $2,965 fee is worth it for time-sensitive situations.
